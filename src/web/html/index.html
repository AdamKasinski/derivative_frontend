Financial derivatives

Derivatives are financial securities whose value is derived from an underlying asset or group of assets. They are contracts between two or more parties, and their price is dependent on changes in the underlying asset, which can be stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies, interest rates, or market indexes. Essentially, derivatives are used for hedging risks or speculating on the future price of an asset. Hedging allows individuals and businesses to protect themselves against price movements that could incur losses. Speculators, on the other hand, take on this risk in the hopes of making a profit.
